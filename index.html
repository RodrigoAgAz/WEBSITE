ROI Calculator Methodology

1. Revenue Impact Calculations

1.1 STAR Rating Revenue (Quality Bonus + Rebate)

Formula:

STAR Improvement = Σ(Improvement% × Measure Weight)
Quality Bonus = Members × $14,000 × 5% (if crossing 4-star threshold)
Rebate Increase = Estimated Savings × (New Rebate% - Current Rebate%)

Key Assumptions:

  • CMS Quality Bonus: 5% of benchmark for plans ≥4.0 stars
  • Rebate percentages: 3.5★=50%, 4.0★=65%, 4.5★=70% of savings
  • Measure weights: Medication Adherence (0.12), AWV (0.08), Readmissions (0.06)
  • Maximum STAR improvement capped at 0.5 stars
1.2 Member Retention Revenue

Formula:

Retained Members = Total Members × Churn Rate × Churn Reduction%
Retention Revenue = Retained Members × (LTV - Acquisition Cost) / LTV Years

Key Assumptions:

  • Member Lifetime Value (LTV): $14,000 × 3.5 years = $49,000
  • Acquisition cost: $750 per new member
  • Plans typically backfill churned members, so we calculate net value
1.3 Annual Wellness Visit (AWV) Revenue

Formula:

Additional AWVs = AWV-Engaged Members × AWV Improvement%
AWV Revenue = Additional AWVs × ($175 CMS payment + $1,500 HCC value)

Key Assumptions:

  • CMS payment per AWV: $175
  • Average HCC risk adjustment value captured: $1,500
  • 60% of SMS-engaged members respond to AWV scheduling

2. Cost Savings Calculations

2.1 Medication Adherence Savings

Formula:

Eligible Members = Med-Engaged Members × 65% (chronic med prevalence)
Effective Improvement = Base Improvement × Diminishing Returns Factor
Savings = Eligible Members × Effective Improvement × $15 per point

Key Assumptions:

  • 65% of Medicare members are on chronic medications
  • $15 saved per percentage point improvement in adherence
  • 70% of SMS-engaged members respond to medication reminders
  • Diminishing returns: 75% effectiveness above 75% baseline, 50% above 85%
2.2 Readmission Prevention

Formula:

At-Risk Members = Discharge-Engaged Members × 20% (admission rate)
Baseline Readmissions = At-Risk Members × 18% (readmission rate)
Prevented Readmissions = Baseline × Readmission Reduction%
Savings = Prevented Readmissions × $15,000

Key Assumptions:

  • 20% annual hospital admission rate
  • 18% 30-day readmission rate baseline
  • $15,000 average cost per readmission
  • 80% of SMS-engaged members respond to discharge follow-ups
2.3 Care Team Efficiency

Formula:

Hours Saved = Total Engaged Members × 2 hours/year
FTE Equivalent = Hours Saved / 2,080 hours
Savings = Hours Saved × $35/hour (fully loaded cost)

Key Assumptions:

  • 2 hours saved annually per engaged member
  • $35/hour fully loaded care team cost
  • 2,080 working hours per FTE annually

3. Engagement & Response Rates

Based on pilot data, different message types achieve different response rates:

  • Medication Reminders: 70% of overall engagement rate
  • AWV Scheduling: 60% of overall engagement rate
  • Post-Discharge Check-ins: 80% of overall engagement rate

Example: If overall SMS engagement is 60%, then 42% respond to medication reminders, 36% complete AWV scheduling, and 48% respond to discharge follow-ups.

4. Overlap Adjustments

To prevent double-counting benefits, we apply the following adjustments:

  • Adherence → Readmissions: 30% of adherence savings already prevent readmissions
  • STAR → Retention: 25% of STAR bonus value already captured in retention
  • AWV → Adherence: 15% of AWV value improves medication tracking
Total Value = Revenue + Savings - Overlap Adjustments

5. Program Investment & ROI

Anna Program Cost:

Annual Investment = Members × $1.00 PMPM × 12 months

Return on Investment:

Net Benefit = Total Value - Anna Investment
ROI% = (Net Benefit / Anna Investment) × 100

8. Limitations & Disclaimers

  • Results are estimates based on industry averages and pilot data
  • Actual results will vary based on member population characteristics
  • STAR rating improvements typically take 12-18 months to fully realize
  • Some benefits may take multiple years to achieve
  • Calculations assume consistent program engagement over time
  • Does not account for potential competitive responses or market changes
